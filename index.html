PHASE CRAFT DIRECTIVES · MINDSET FRAMEWORKS · REJECTION PROTOCOL · IDENTITY UNDER PRESSURE
PHASE 1 — DAYS 1–30 · FOUNDATION
Focus on neutral accent fundamentals — 20 min daily minimum
3 scenes minimum per week — fully memorised, not just read
On-camera technique over performance — learn the lens first
Diction exercises daily — AEIOU vowel work every morning
Self-tape workflow established — light, sound, framing locked
Begin 9 Rasa emotional range work — one Rasa per session
PHASE 2 — DAYS 31–60 · INTENSITY
Shift to character specificity — motivation, history, physicality
Increase self-tape output — minimum 2 per week submitted
Dialect pressure testing — perform in accent under fatigue
Cold reading drills — sight-read + perform within 90 seconds
Script analysis weekly — break down structure, subtext, arc
Camera improvisation — single-take unscripted presence work
PHASE 3 — DAYS 61–90 · MASTERY
Full audition simulation weekly — casting director conditions
Stunt + performance integration — physicality in every scene
Monologue mastery — 8 memorised, performance-ready
Professional self-tape submission — real roles, real pipelines
Reel assembly — curate 3 best takes into demo footage
REJECTION PROTOCOL — READ WHEN IT HURTS
THE TRUTH ABOUT REJECTION IN THIS INDUSTRY
Rejection is not information about your worth. It is information about the casting director's current need. You were not seen — you were filtered. The role you didn't get was already cast three weeks ago before auditions opened. Your job is not to book — your job is to be so undeniable that the one who does see you cannot say no. Every rejection is a rep. You are building your audition fitness. Amateurs treat rejection as verdict. Professionals treat rejection as volume. Keep the volume high. Keep the craft sharp. The booking comes to those still standing.
